About

I am a software engineer at Amazon.com (no views or opinions expressed in this site reflect the views or opinions of my employer). I have experience in computer graphics, distributed backend services, and frontend development. I also have experience working as a Lighting Technical Assistant at DreamWorks Animation. I received my Masters degree at the Computer Science and Engineering department in UC San Diego on 2016 studying VR and HCI under Jurgen Schulze and Scott Klemmer. My B.S. in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science hails from UC Berkeley.

My technical interests span human computer interaction, computer graphics, especially in computer animated movies, and am pursuing this both in my career and hobbies. From a humanities side, I am interested in visual narrative, and storytelling. I marry these interests by working on narrative and entertainment technology.

Both stemming from and feeding into my career interest, I love to draw, take photos, and play the piano, in my hobby time. You can find my recent works at my Doodle Blog or my flickr page.

Projects

Chromatography

Chromatography is an exploration of visualizing and analyzing complex, abstract information from film such as color palette through information visualization principles in order to enable the film maker, critic or historian to compare and contrast multiple films at the same time. An interactive iPython Notebook Prototype was built with extracted information to allow a user to interact with these elements.

Paper: PDF

PhysLights

A novel approach to CG lighting named PhysLights. PhysLights enables lighting artists to execute their vision and artistic choices in the physical space through a Tangible User Interface. This lighting is then represented in a conventional 3D package and rendered. This was done with a 3D printed character, an identical virtual 3D model, and spot lights that were tracked through Microsoft Kinect.

Paper: PDF

Reflection space Image Based Rendering

We implemented Reflection Space Image-Base Rendering as described by Cabral et al. in Reflection space image based rendering. HDR environment maps contain high-quality lighting information, but integrating them is costly, especially if view-dependent materials are involved. Our goal is to generate a scene in which the user can rotate around the object lit by an HDR environment map, and observe view dependent BRDF changes in real-time.

Paper: PDF

Visualization of Dataflow Analyses

In Visualization of Dataflow Analyses, we explored displaying dataflow analysis in a visual manner juxtaposed with the code. In order to make this happen we decided to build a small language on top of the blockly framework and display our dataflow analysis alongside it.
