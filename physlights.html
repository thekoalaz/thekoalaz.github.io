PhysLights

By Kevin Lim, Bryan Binotti

Abstract

We present a novel approach to CG lighting named PhysLights. PhysLights enables lighting artists to execute their vision and artistic choices in the physical space through a Tangible User Interface. This lighting is then represented in a conventional 3D package and rendered. Through PhysLights we explore the advantages and disadvantages of a Tangible User Interface in CG Lighting workflows, and the extent in which it can augment or replace traditional pipelines. PhysLights is part of our vision to make CG Animation production more approachable, more collaborative, and improve pre-existing pipelines.

Overview

Based on my experience as a Lighting Technical Assistant at DreamWorks Animation, PhysLights was created to see if there were more intuitive approaches to CG lighting where the lighting artist, director, or cinematographer can become closer to the final render.

We built a Tangible User Interface prototype for lighting, PhysLights as an attempt to evaluate how effective such a system can be. Then we tested the system with DreamWorks Animation lighters.
